## Governance

### Chair
- Leads trustee meetings and sets the agenda
- Holds casting vote when trustees are deadlocked
- Acts as final escalation point for serious complaints, incidents, and decisions
- Represents the charity externally in an official capacity
- Ensures trustees fulfil their legal duties under Charity Commission requirements
- Signs off on policy suite publication and annual review
- *Charity Commission requirement*

### Treasurer
- Manages day-to-day charity finances
- Maintains financial records and bank accounts
- Authorises expenditure within agreed thresholds
- Prepares financial reports for trustee meetings
- Submits annual accounts to the Charity Commission
- Manages Gift Aid claims where applicable
- Ensures financial records are retained for 6 years (HMRC requirement)
- *Charity Commission requirement*

### Data Protection Lead
- Acts as the charity's named contact for all GDPR matters
- Handles subject access requests, erasure requests, and rectification requests from individuals
- Reports data breaches to the ICO within 72 hours where required
- Ensures data retention periods are followed and data is deleted on schedule
- Advises trustees and fosterers on data handling queries
- Reviews the Privacy Notice and data policies annually
- *UK GDPR requirement — must be a named individual*

### Safeguarding Lead
- Acts as the charity's named safeguarding contact
- Recognises and responds to safeguarding concerns involving children or vulnerable adults
- Escalates concerns to statutory agencies (police, social services) where required
- Maintains awareness of Welsh Government safeguarding guidance and the Social Services and Well-being (Wales) Act 2014
- Ensures all trustees and fosterers know how to report a concern
- Reviews the Safeguarding Policy annually
- *Charity Commission requirement — must be a named individual*

### Lottery Licence Holder
- Named individual on the charity's Gambling Act 2005 lottery licence
- Ensures lottery draws comply with licence conditions (prize limits, ticket pricing, age verification)
- Maintains records of all draws, ticket sales, prize distribution, and proceeds
- Ensures lottery terms and conditions are publicly accessible
- Renews the licence as required
- *Gambling Act 2005 requirement — must be a named individual on the licence*

### Policy Manager
- Maintains and updates the charity's policy suite
- Ensures all policies are version-controlled and review dates are tracked
- Coordinates the annual policy review with trustees
- Stores the authoritative copies of all policies in the designated location
- Ensures fosterers and adopters receive the correct policy documents

### Crisis / Media Contact
- Acts as the charity's spokesperson if an incident becomes public
- Manages the charity's response to negative press, social media incidents, or public complaints
- Coordinates with the Chair on messaging
- Follows the Social Media Policy crisis response procedure

## Cat Care Operations

### Cat Intake Lead
- Formally accepts cats into the charity's care
- Ensures intake procedure is followed: microchip scan, health assessment, vaccination, records created
- Coordinates with the fosterer receiving the cat for transport and placement
- Handles owner relinquishment intake using the Cat Relinquish Policy
- *ADCH Standards 1–4, 76*

### Microchipping Officer
- Performs microchip scans on intake using an ISO-compliant scanner
- Registers microchips with an appropriate UK database with the organisation listed as keeper
- Updates chip registration at the point of adoption to the new owner
- *ADCH Standards 1–2*

### Flea & Wormer Lead
- Administers or coordinates flea and wormer treatments for cats in care
- Ensures treatments are applied on schedule and recorded
- Stores veterinary products securely per COSHH requirements
- *ADCH Standard 52*

### Food Ordering
- Coordinates food purchasing for the foster network
- Ensures stock levels are maintained and food is appropriate for cats' ages and conditions
- *ADCH Standard 5*

### Vet Liaison
- Acts as the charity's primary point of contact with the nominated vet practice(s)
- Coordinates vet appointments, health checks, and emergency consultations
- Ensures every cat receives a vet examination while in care
- Maintains the list of nominated vet practices
- Arranges documented isolation/quarantine capacity with the vet practice for infectious cats
- *ADCH Standards 3, 51, 13*

### Home Checker / Foster Home Assessor
- Visits (in person or virtually) and formally assesses every foster home before first use
- Confirms the home environment meets ADCH Annex B minimum housing standards
- Confirms the foster carer is competent to meet a cat's welfare needs
- Maintains a record of every assessed and approved foster home
- Re-assesses homes if circumstances change significantly
- *ADCH Standard 60 — must be a named, suitably knowledgeable individual*

### Isolation Coordinator
- Ensures a documented arrangement is in place with a vet practice for isolation of infectious cats
- Coordinates placement of cats with suspected infectious disease
- *ADCH Standard 13*

## Fostering & Adoptions

### Foster Coordinator
- Matches cats to appropriate foster homes based on the cat's needs and fosterer capacity
- Coordinates adoption visits between fosterers and prospective adopters
- Supports fosterers with day-to-day queries and welfare concerns
- Escalates welfare concerns, complaints, or incidents to the Chair
- Ensures fosterers have access to current policies and have signed acknowledgement forms
- Acts as on-call contact for fosterers during their assigned period
- *Multiple people may share this role*

### Adoption Approvals Lead
- Reviews adoption applications and makes the final decision to approve or decline
- Ensures proof of address is obtained from all adopters
- Ensures adopters receive care advice, vet history, and behavioural information at handover
- Ensures all cats are vaccinated, treated for parasites, and examined before adoption
- Maintains adoption records per the Data Retention Policy
- *ADCH Standards 57, 71–74*

### On-Call Contact for Fosterers
- Provides a named point of contact for fosterers outside normal hours
- Responds to welfare emergencies, escape incidents, or unexpected cat deaths
- Escalates to the Chair or Vet Liaison where required
- *Rota-based if shared across Foster Coordinators*

### Behavioural Support Contact
- Provides or coordinates access to a suitably trained individual for cats exhibiting unwanted behaviour
- Ensures all training and behaviour modification uses positive reinforcement only
- *ADCH Standard 55 — can be an external behaviourist; a named contact must be documented*
