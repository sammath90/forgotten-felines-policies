# SOP 3: Existing Member External Assessment

**Source:** https://adch.org.uk/minimum-standards/existing-member-external-assessment-standard-operating-procedure-sop-3/
**Organisation:** Association of Dogs and Cats Homes (ADCH)

---

## Overview

This document outlines ADCH's process for conducting external assessments of member organisations, designed with transparency for both current and prospective members.

---

## Key Definitions

**Site/Centre:** A single location or group of locations housing animals under common management, which may or may not be owned by the rescue organisation.

**Foster Carer:** Typically operates from a domestic home, providing temporary care for animals. These may be housed in the home or purpose-built facilities on private property.

**SAWC:** Standards and Animal Welfare Committee, which has delegated authority from the Trustees to make decisions on Membership.

---

## Assessment Procedure

### 1. Independent Assessments
External assessments are conducted by independent assessors rather than staff or volunteers employed by member organisations.

### 2. Assessment Schedule
Over time, every member will be assessed at least once every three years, with external assessments potentially occurring alongside self-assessments.

### 3. Selection and Appointment
SAWC determines which members undergo assessment annually. The Board or SAWC appoints one or two assessors to complete evaluations within established timescales. Assessors represent ADCH's interests during evaluations.

### 4. Reporting Requirements
Assessors submit completed forms, action plans for non-compliant areas, and recommendations to the designated ADCH email or online portal. Members receive copies of assessment forms automatically.

### 5. Committee Review
SAWC receives comprehensive reports detailing all non-compliance areas identified by assessors and members.

### 6. Persistent Non-Compliance
Members failing to facilitate assessments face procedures outlined in SOP 7.

### 7. Remedial Action Plans
Non-compliant members must submit remedial plans for SAWC review. Organisations without submitted plans may face membership termination recommendations to the Board of Trustees.

### 8. Multi-Site Members
Organisations with multiple locations should reference SOP 4 for specific guidance.
