# SOP 8: Revision of ADCH's Minimum Welfare and Operational Standards

**Source:** https://adch.org.uk/minimum-standards/standards-revision-sop8/
**Organisation:** Association of Dogs and Cats Homes (ADCH)

---

## Key Definitions

**SAWC:** ADCH's Standards and Animal Welfare Committee, which possesses delegated authority from the Trustees to make recommendations regarding Standards.

---

## 1. Introduction

1.1 The ADCH Minimum Welfare and Operational Standards were created and approved by Trustees and Members. These standards establish membership requirements that:
- Protect animal welfare
- Preserve the reputation of member organisations, ADCH, and the sector
- Remain proportionate and achievable

1.2 All Members must participate in the assessment process and commit to addressing areas of non-compliance.

1.3 SAWC recommends changes to the Board of Trustees regarding standards revisions. The Committee may make minor clarifications without Board approval.

1.4 This SOP explains how SAWC executes these responsibilities.

---

## 2. Procedure

2.1 SAWC maintains a running log of topics requiring potential attention, including standard updates, new standards creation, and existing standard deletion.

2.2 Standards development incorporates input from:
- Member experiences
- Assessor feedback during member evaluations
- SAWC review of assessments
- Scientific and legislative developments
- Changes in organisational operating models
- Clarification needs for existing standards
- Other relevant considerations

2.3 SAWC conducts reviews to determine revision necessity no later than 2 years after the latest revision, then annually if no revision occurred.

2.4 SAWC determines revision timing based on relevant factors, prioritising legislative alignment.

2.5 SAWC organises revision work as deemed appropriate.

2.6 SAWC distributes consultation proposals to Full Members, allowing adequate response time based on proposed changes' complexity.

2.7 SAWC reviews consultation responses and determines appropriate amendments.

2.8 SAWC presents recommendations to Trustees for approval.

2.9 Upon approval, SAWC publishes results to members with sufficient implementation time and may set different dates for different standards.

2.10 SAWC publishes new standards with implementation dates.
