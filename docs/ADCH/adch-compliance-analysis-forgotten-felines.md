# ADCH Compliance Analysis — Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue

**Prepared:** 2026-04-06
**Charity No.:** 1181190
**Model:** Foster-based, cats only, no physical premises
**Standards version:** ADCH Minimum Welfare & Operational Standards, August 2024

---

## Overview

ADCH membership is open to foster-based rescues — the standards explicitly accommodate the model (Standard 59, 60, 62, 43, 47). Forgotten Felines does not need premises to join. The foster room replaces the cattery unit for housing standards (Annex B). Being solutions-focused, ADCH supports smaller rescues in meeting standards rather than simply rejecting those with gaps.

This analysis covers: which standards apply, what you already have, what you're missing, and the steps to achieve membership.

---

## Standards That Don't Apply

The following are **excluded** for Forgotten Felines (cats only, no physical premises):

- **Standards 8, 10:** Communal feeding areas; on-premises food preparation facilities — not applicable in foster homes
- **Standards 32–36:** Dog-specific housing and behaviour standards
- **Standard 14:** First aid kit requirement — applies to premises with more than 5 pens; not applicable in individual foster homes
- **Annex A (A1–A17):** Dogs housing conditions
- **Standards 19:** Waste management for premises
- **Annex C (C1–C9):** Importation — only relevant if you import cats from outside UK/Ireland

### Group 6: Training (Standard 48) — CRITICAL GAP

**Standard 48** is one of the most significant requirements for a foster-based rescue:

> *"In foster homes, the foster carer must be trained and given guidance by the organisation. Training must cover:"*

1. Animal welfare, including recognising poor welfare
2. Animal handling
3. Animal behaviour
4. Cleanliness and hygiene
5. Feeding and food preparation
6. Disease control
7. Recognition and first aid action for sick animals
8. New information on disease and inherited disease, legislation and behaviour

> *"The training policy should be reviewed and updated annually. Training records for individual staff and volunteers should be retained."*

**Current status:** ⛔ **Not documented.** Training almost certainly happens informally — but ADCH requires a documented training programme with individual records.

**What you need:**
- A written fosterer training programme covering all 8 topics
- A record system showing each fosterer has completed training
- Annual review of the training content
- This is likely delivered through: your **Volunteer Policy**, **Fostering Policy**, and **Cats in Care Policy** combined — but you also need a training record/log

### Group 7: Foster Home Standards (Standards 59–64) — CRITICAL

| Standard | Requirement | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 59 | Foster homes must meet Annex B (cats housing conditions) | ⚠ See detail below |
| 60 | Every foster home visited/assessed and approved before use | ⚠ Home checks — needs documented process |
| 62 | Cats from different sources not mixed; confined to easily cleanable room; no mixing with resident cats | ⚠ Needs to be explicit in policy |
| 63 | Dogs not left alone long enough to become distressed | N/A (cats only) |
| 64 | N/A (cats only) |

**Standard 59 — Annex B in foster homes:**
The foster "room" must meet these minimum conditions:
- **B8:** Cat can move to cooler area if hot ✓
- **B9:** Hiding place; sleeping area ≥0.85m² for 1 cat (roughly 90cm × 90cm)
- **B10:** Raised areas/shelves for cats to rest up high ✓
- **B11–B14:** Heating; minimum 10°C, part at 15°C; no open flames ✓
- **B15:** Suitable washable bedding ✓
- **B16:** Play/exercise space ≥1.65m² for 1 cat (roughly 130cm × 130cm); minimum ceiling height 1.8m ✓ (most rooms)
- **B17:** Hiding place (box, igloo bed, blanket) ✓

**Practical implication:** The designated foster room needs to be large enough (~1.65m² usable floor space for one cat), have a raised surface, a hiding space, washable surfaces and bedding, and be able to be kept at 15°C+. Most spare bedrooms or utility rooms would meet this.

**Standard 60 — Foster home assessment:**
You need a documented process for visiting/assessing homes before cats are placed. This assessment must confirm:
- Home environment is suitable
- Foster carer is competent to meet the cat's needs

**Current status:** You may do informal home checks but need:
- A written home check/assessment process
- Records of each home assessed
- A defined approval status

## ADCH Membership Roadmap

### Phase 1: Get your house in order (Before applying)

**Step 1 — Write the required policies** (most are already in your PRD Phase 1 scope):
- Cats in Care Policy *(includes intake, vet checks, feeding, monitoring, isolation)*
- Fosterer Cleaning Policy *(includes cleaning standards, disinfectants, litter)*
- Transportation of a Cat Policy *(Standard 54)*
- Escape of a Cat Policy *(Standard 78)*
- Death of a Cat Policy *(Standard 78)*
- Euthanasia Policy *(Standard 70)*
- Fostering Policy *(includes training requirements, home assessment, Standard 62)*
- Adoption Policy *(includes rehoming standards 71–75, neutering)*

**Step 2 — Establish your records system:**
- A per-cat record log (spreadsheet is fine — description, intake date, vet history, assessments, adopter details)
- Fosterer training records (log of who was trained and when, against the 8 topics)
- Foster home assessment records (one record per approved home)

**Step 3 — Document your named vet practice(s):**
- Write down your nominated vet(s) and confirm they can provide isolation if needed

### Phase 2: Self-assessment

**Step 4 — Complete the ADCH self-assessment** (Step 3 of SOP1):
- Score yourself against each applicable standard
- Identify any remaining gaps
- ADCH assessors will also do this with you — it's collaborative, not a gotcha

### Phase 3: Apply

**Step 5 — Submit application to ADCH:**
- Online application or email [email protected]
- Need: 2 references (existing ADCH members or professional refs — your vet qualifies)
- Need: Organisational constitution
- Need: 2 years financial accounts (or bank statements if newer)

**Step 6 — Assessment visit:**
- ADCH arranges assessment within 6–8 weeks
- For a foster-based rescue, assessors will visit at least one foster home (can be virtual — video call + Google Earth)
- Assessment is solutions-focused — they want to help you pass, not catch you out

**Step 7 — Conditional admission if needed:**
- If minor gaps remain, ADCH can admit conditionally pending evidenced improvements

### Phase 4: Ongoing compliance

- **Annual self-assessment** (SOP2)
- **External assessment every 3 years** (SOP3)
- **Training policy reviewed annually** (Standard 48)
- **Emergency plan reviewed annually** (Standard 46)

## Positive News for Forgotten Felines

1. **Your PRD scope already covers most of the required written procedures.** The 8 policies in Phase 1 that relate to cat care (Cats in Care, Cleaning, Transport, Escape, Death of Cat, Euthanasia, Fostering, Adoption) map almost perfectly to what ADCH requires under Standard 78 and Standard 70.

2. **Foster-based is explicitly supported.** ADCH doesn't require premises. Standards 43, 47, 59, 60, and 62 are written with foster homes in mind.

3. **Annex B is achievable.** The space requirements (1.65m² usable for one cat, raised surface, hiding place, washable room) are met by a standard bedroom or spare room. Most of your foster homes likely already meet this informally.

4. **The main gap is documentation, not practice.** Things like microchipping, vet checks, and daily care are almost certainly happening already — ADCH needs to see that you have written procedures and records to prove it.

5. **Your two biggest new requirements** beyond the policy suite are:
   - A **fosterer training record system** (who was trained, on what, when)
   - A **per-cat record log** (a Google Sheet would be sufficient)
