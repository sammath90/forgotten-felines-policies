# SOP 4: Assessment of Members and Applicants with Multiple Sites

**Source:** https://adch.org.uk/minimum-standards/assessment-of-members-and-applicants-with-multiple-sites-standard-operating-procedure-sop-4/
**Organisation:** Association of Dogs and Cats Homes (ADCH)

---

## Overview

This standard operating procedure establishes guidelines for assessing member organisations and applicants operating four or more sites.

---

## Key Definitions

**Site/Centre:** A single location housing animals, or multiple locations under common management (may or may not be owned by the rescue).

**Foster Carer:** Typically domestic homes providing temporary animal care, sometimes with purpose-built facilities on private property.

**SAWC:** Standards and Animal Welfare Committee with delegated authority to make membership decisions.

---

## Two-Phase Assessment Structure

### Phase 1: Head Office Review

Conducted by two trained assessors (one designated as lead), this phase examines:

- Quality assurance and monitoring systems
- Standard operating procedures and policies
- Staff and volunteer training implementation
- Management supervision processes

**Quality Assurance Assessment includes:**
Organisations must demonstrate how their standards align with ADCH's minimum standards, maintain updated data systems, establish clear management reporting lines, and recognise animal welfare risks.

**Training and Supervision:** Assessors verify that management can identify compliance levels with ADCH standards and that remedial actions are undertaken when needed.

### Phase 2: Site Visits

Purpose is to validate the outputs from the Member's own QA system reporting. Different assessors typically conduct these visits, with one assessor per site.

---

## Site Visit Numbers Guide (External Assessment)

| Total Sites | Visits Required |
|---|---|
| Fewer than 4 | Most sites; no HQ visit required |
| 4–30 | HQ visit plus 3 sites |
| 31–50 | HQ visit plus 10% of total sites |
| Over 50 | HQ visit plus agreed number |

---

## Self-Assessment Requirements

Similar structure with varying site visit numbers based on organisation size, ranging from all sites for organisations under 4 locations to negotiated numbers for organisations exceeding 51 sites.

---

## Key Principles

- Different sites selected during each assessment cycle
- All site types represented in visits
- "Best and worst" principle applied when selecting locations
- Licensed boarding premises compliance verified separately
- Phase 1 report shared with member for accuracy checking before SAWC review
