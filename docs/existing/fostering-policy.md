# Fostering Policy

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

Cats and kittens are cared for in foster homes until a suitable home is found.

---

## Housing & Separation

- Foster cats must be kept separate from other cats that may be in the household until fully vaccinated.
- Any owned cats in the household must already be neutered and fully vaccinated prior to the arrival of a foster cat/kitten.
- Foster cats will remain indoors. They are not allowed to roam outside.

### A single foster room will only house:

- Maximum of 3 adult cats relinquished from the same household
- A mother cat with her own kittens
- A litter of kittens from the same household

---

## Daily Care

- The fosterer will undertake daily checks on the foster cats including rear/tail, eyes, ears, nose, mouth, body, and limbs.
- The fosterer will spend time with the foster cat on a daily basis to ensure they are happy and socialised.
- Fresh water will always be available.
- Good quality food appropriate to the age of the cat will be provided.
- Biodegradable litter will be provided.
- Beds and places to hide will be provided for the cat.
- A number of different levels will be provided in the room for the cat to explore.
- Toys and activities will be provided to help keep the cat entertained.

---

## Cleaning

- Daily cleaning using Anigiene disinfectant (or similar).
- Deep cleaning is carried out weekly, including deep cleaning of litter trays, bedding, toys, shelters, and food bowls.

---

## Veterinary Care

- Cats will receive Minimum Veterinary Standards (MVS) as soon as practically possible once delivered to the foster home. MVS includes: microchipping, neutering, treatment for parasites, and vaccinations.
- High risk, sick cats, or cats where advised by a vet, will be blood tested for FIV/FeLV.
- All cats are neutered at 4 months of age.

---

## Emergency & Contact

- Each fosterer has the contact details of the Fostering Coordinator for immediate advice.
- If an emergency occurs, the fosterer must contact their fosterer coordinator for authority for a vet visit.
- If a fosterer coordinator or Trustee cannot be located and an emergency arises, the fosterer has authority to contact the local vet.

---

## Transport

All cats and kittens are transported using a secure basket with a fitted cover. All fosterers are provided with a MDC carrier and fitted cover for transportation.

---

## Home Assessment & Agreement

- The Fostering Coordinator visits any prospective fosterer to ensure they can provide a suitable environment for a cat.
- A fostering agreement will be signed by the fosterer.
- Each foster home is visited annually to ensure standards are maintained.

---

*This policy will be reviewed annually.*
