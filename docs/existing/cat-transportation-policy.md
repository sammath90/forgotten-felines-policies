# Transportation of a Cat Policy

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

Most cats find travel stressful. It is important for all journeys to be kept as calm and short as possible. No cat is to be transported in a way that causes injury or unnecessary suffering.

---

## Carrier Standards

- All cats and kittens are transported in a secure, well ventilated, sturdy top-loading carrier
- Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue supply all volunteers that transport cats with an easy-to-clean metal wire carrier together with a carrier cover
- The carrier is large enough for the cat to stand and turn
- The carrier is lined with familiar bedding

---

## Preparing for the Journey

- Feliway (or similar) can be sprayed 15 minutes before travel if the cat is nervous
- Leave the carrier in the foster room so the cat can explore it beforehand
- Add blankets, treats, or toys
- Avoid forcing the cat in unless absolutely necessary
- Plan the journey and keep it as short as possible

---

## During the Journey

- The carrier should be level and secure
- Avoid loud noises, sudden movements, or extreme temperatures
- Never leave the cat alone in the vehicle
- Never allow the cat to travel loose in the vehicle
- Secure the carrier with a seatbelt
- Avoid opening the carrier during the journey
- Check the cat regularly on longer trips
- Monitor the cat for stress or motion sickness

---

## After the Journey

- Clean and disinfect carriers after each use
- Report any incidents (vomiting, escape attempts, injury) to the fosterer coordinator
- Paperwork or digital records must travel with the cat

---

*This policy will be reviewed annually.*
