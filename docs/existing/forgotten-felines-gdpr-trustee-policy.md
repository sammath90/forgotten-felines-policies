# GDPR & Data Protection Policy — Trustees / Official Communications

**Purpose:** To ensure trustees handle personal information lawfully, fairly, and securely when communicating with adopters, supporters, volunteers, and partners.

---

## 1. Principles

Forgotten Felines trustees must follow GDPR principles:

- Collect only the information needed to run rescue operations.
- Use information only for legitimate rescue purposes.
- Keep all personal data secure.
- Share information strictly on a need-to-know basis.
- Delete information when it is no longer required.

## 2. Approved Communication Channels

To ensure security, consistency, and proper record-keeping, trustees must only communicate using official Forgotten Felines channels:

- Facebook Page & Messenger
- Instagram Messages
- WhatsApp Business / Official WhatsApp Groups
- Official Forgotten Felines Email (if applicable)

Personal accounts, personal phone numbers, or unofficial WhatsApp chats must not be used for adopter communication unless unavoidable and approved. In such cases, conversations must be moved to official channels as soon as possible.

### Communication Between Adopters & Fosterers

- The adopter's name and phone number may be shared with the fosterer once the adopter is approved.
- Fosterers may contact adopters via phone call or text message only.
- Fosterers must use the adopter's number solely for coordinating the meet/collection.
- Fosterers must not store adopter details long-term and must delete them after adoption unless needed for welfare follow-up.

## 3. Handling Personal Information

Trustees may access or store:

- Names
- Phone numbers
- Email addresses
- Home addresses
- Adoption application details
- Shared photos/videos
- Volunteer information

Trustees must keep this information confidential, secure, and used only for rescue activities.

## 4. Social Media & Public Sharing

- No personal details of adopters or volunteers may be posted publicly.
- Photos containing identifiable people must not be shared without consent.
- "Happy adoption" photos require consent from the adopter.

## 5. Data Retention

Data should be kept only as long as legally or operationally required. Outdated notes or contact details must be deleted.

## 6. Breach Reporting

Any suspected data breach must be reported immediately to the Chair or Data Protection Lead.
