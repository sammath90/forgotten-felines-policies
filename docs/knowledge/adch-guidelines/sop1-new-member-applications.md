# SOP 1: New Member Applications

**Source:** https://adch.org.uk/minimum-standards/new-member-applications-standard-operating-procedure-sop-1/
**Organisation:** Association of Dogs and Cats Homes (ADCH)

---

## Overview

This document outlines ADCH's transparent process for evaluating organisations seeking membership. The procedure emphasises openness and fair assessment of prospective members.

---

## Key Definitions

**Site/Centre:** A single location or multiple locations housing animals under unified management, which may or may not be owned by the rescue organisation.

**Foster Carer:** Typically operates from a domestic home providing temporary animal care. Foster homes can house animals directly or maintain purpose-built facilities on private property.

**SAWC:** Standards and Animal Welfare Committee, which holds delegated authority from Trustees regarding membership decisions.

---

## Application Process (16 Steps)

1. **Initial Submission:** Prospective members complete applications online or request forms via mail/email from the Standards and Operations Officer.

2. **References Required:** Applicants must provide two existing ADCH members as endorsers, or alternatively two professional references such as veterinarians or local authority representatives.

3. **Self-Assessment:** Organisations evaluate themselves against ADCH's Minimum Welfare and Operational Standards.

4. **Documentation:** Required supporting materials include organisational constitutions and two years of financial accounts (or recent bank statements for newly formed organisations).

5. **Officer Review:** The Standards and Operations Officer verifies receipt, contacts referees, notifies Trustees of concerns, and lists applications on the members-only website for confidential feedback.

6. **Assessment Timeline:** Evaluations typically occur within 6–8 weeks. Applicants must cooperate scheduling site visits and confirm dates within six weeks or face application termination.

7. **Assessment Submission:** Assessors complete forms highlighting any areas scoring below two, submitted within seven days of visits.

8. **Remediation Plans:** Applications include applicants' strategies for addressing deficiencies; complex cases may be postponed pending facility improvements.

9. **SAWC Review:** Committee approves, declines, or refers applications to the Board of Trustees.

10. **Membership Approval:** Accepted applicants receive written confirmation; the Bookkeeper issues invoices based on current fee schedules and pro-rata calculations.

11. **Conditional Admission:** SAWC may temporarily pause membership pending required improvements, with admittance proceeding after evidenced changes.

12. **Multi-Site Applications:** Organisations with multiple locations follow additional procedures outlined in SOP 4.

13. **Declined Applications:** Rejections follow procedures detailed in SOP 7.
