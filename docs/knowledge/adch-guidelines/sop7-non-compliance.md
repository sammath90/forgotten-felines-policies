# SOP 7: Non-Compliance with ADCH's Minimum Welfare and Operational Standards

**Source:** https://adch.org.uk/minimum-standards/non-compliance-with-adchs-minimum-welfare-and-operational-standards-standard-operating-procedure-sop-7/
**Organisation:** Association of Dogs and Cats Homes (ADCH)

---

## Key Definitions

**Site/Centre:** A single location or group of locations housing animals under common management.

**Foster Carer:** Typically a domestic home providing temporary animal care, potentially in purpose-built facilities on private property.

**SAWC:** ADCH's Standards and Animal Welfare Committee with delegated authority to make membership decisions.

---

## 1. Introduction

The standards aim to safeguard animal welfare; safeguard the reputation of member organisations, ADCH and the sector; and are proportionate and realistically achievable. All members must engage with assessment processes and remedy non-compliance.

---

## 2. Procedures

ADCH addresses three applicant/member groups:

- New applicants not meeting standards
- Existing members non-compliant with assessment requirements
- Existing members failing to meet standards

The organisation provides support including one-to-one sessions, site visits, grant funding signposting, training opportunities, and peer mentoring ("Buddying").

---

## 3. New Applicant Non-Compliance

SAWC considers whether non-compliance is partial or complete, reviews proposed action plans, assesses feasibility and timelines, and evaluates animal welfare risks. Declined applications include appeal rights through the Board of Trustees.

---

## 4. Assessment Non-Compliance

Members must complete assessments within eight weeks. After three notification attempts without compliance, SAWC may recommend membership termination, considering offered support, time since previous assessment, and other mandatory engagement factors.

---

## 5. Existing Member Non-Compliance

Non-compliant members create action plans with remediation timescales. SAWC reviews plans, may request further investigation or assessments, and can recommend termination if standards remain unmet after agreed periods.

---

## 6. Failure to Comply

SAWC examines special circumstances (financial or staffing issues), progress on action points, extension feasibility, and organisational motivation. If insufficient improvement is evident, SAWC recommends membership termination to Trustees.

---

## 7. Outcomes

The Board may either:

- Transfer members to **provisional status** (maximum 12 months to demonstrate improvement), or
- **Terminate membership**, preventing reapplication for 12 months.

---

## 8. Constitutional Requirements

Termination decisions must follow ADCH constitution procedures, including informing members of reasons, allowing representation timeframes, and ensuring fair consideration at trustee meetings.

---

## 9. Definitions

**Provisional Membership:** Grants full rights except voting, used when compliance evidence is unavailable due to circumstances beyond ADCH control or when additional time is deemed appropriate.

**Action Plans:** Member-created remediation strategies with specific timescales addressing non-compliance areas.
