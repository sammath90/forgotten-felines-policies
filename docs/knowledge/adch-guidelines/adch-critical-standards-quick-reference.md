# ADCH Critical Standards Quick Reference

**Prepared:** 2026-04-11  
**Purpose:** Fast reference for the ADCH standards that most directly affect current Forgotten Felines compliance work.

---

## Role and Governance Dependencies

- **Named role ownership is mandatory** for key operational and safeguarding functions.
- Priority open assignments should always be visible in the roles register and ADCH checklist.

Current operational priorities:

- Lottery Licence Holder
- Cat Intake Lead
- Isolation Coordinator (can be same as Vet Liaison if explicitly assigned)
- Behavioural Support Contact

---

## High-Impact Operational Standards (Current Workstream)

- **Standard 13**: Isolation arrangements and vet support for infectious disease scenarios
- **Standard 48**: Fosterer training programme + individual training records
- **Standard 51**: Nominated vet practice details documented and current
- **Standard 54**: Safe cat transport requirements
- **Standard 55**: Behavioural support contact/resource defined
- **Standard 60**: Foster home assessment process + records
- **Standard 62**: Source-separation requirements in fostering/cats-in-care controls
- **Standards 65-66**: Neutering obligations and controls
- **Standards 70**: Standalone euthanasia policy
- **Standards 71-75**: Adoption controls (health information, handover standards, return expectations)
- **Standard 76**: Per-cat records
- **Standard 78**: Incident policies (escape/death etc.) and emergency readiness

---

## Minimum "Assessment-Ready" Output Set

To be practically ADCH-ready, ensure:

1. Policies exist and are internally consistent.
2. Named role owners are assigned (not `[TBC]`) for critical functions.
3. Evidence records exist (not just policy statements).
4. Annual review cadence and ownership are explicit.

Use `adch-evidence-pack-checklist.md` to build this set.
