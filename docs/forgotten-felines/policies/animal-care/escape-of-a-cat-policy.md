# Escape of a Cat Policy

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue follows a structured response to ensure the cat's safety and to support the fosterer.

---

## Immediate Response

1. The fosterer contacts the fosterer coordinator or Trustee immediately.

2. The fosterer is advised to check the house to ensure the cat has actually escaped and is not hiding — cats can often hide in small places, under sofas, etc.

3. Search the immediate area calmly and quietly.

4. Check the area including gardens, sheds, under decking, garages, bushes, and sheltered spots.

5. Leave the cat's carrier, bedding, litter tray, or familiar scent near the escape point.

6. Avoid chasing or calling loudly, which can push a frightened cat further away.

7. If appropriate, set a humane trap and set up trail cameras.

---

## Wider Search

8. Check that microchip records are up to date and notify the microchip company that the cat is missing.

9. If the cat is missing for more than a short period: notify local vets, local rescues, and lost-and-found networks; post on Facebook and other social media sites.

---

## Recording & Review

10. The incident is recorded in the cat's file.

11. The fosterer is offered guidance and emotional support.

12. Once the cat is found, update records and review the circumstances to prevent future escapes.

13. Review the foster environment; offer advice on safe room setup and double-door systems.

The aim is always to learn and improve safety, not to blame the volunteer.

---

*This policy will be reviewed annually.*
