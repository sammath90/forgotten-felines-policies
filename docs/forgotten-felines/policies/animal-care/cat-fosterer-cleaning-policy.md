## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the minimum cleaning, hygiene, and infection control standards required for all foster carers working with Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue. Its aim is to protect the health and welfare of cats, prevent the spread of disease, and maintain a safe home environment.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all foster carers, volunteers, and temporary caregivers housing cats or kittens on behalf of Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue.

---

## 3. General Hygiene Principles

Foster carers must:

- Maintain a clean, safe, and hygienic environment at all times
- Wash hands thoroughly before and after handling each cat
- Use separate equipment where possible for different cats or litters
- Minimise cross-contamination between foster animals and resident pets
- Follow any isolation instructions provided by the rescue

### 3.1 Hygiene and welfare balance (Standard 15)

Cleaning practice must balance hygiene with the cat's psychological welfare. A foster room that is clinically spotless but stripped of all familiar scent is stressful for a cat, especially during the first 7–14 days of a placement. Foster carers should:

- Remove soiled bedding and wash items promptly, but **avoid cleaning every scented item at the same time**
- Where multiple hides, beds, or blankets are in use, rotate washing so at least one familiar-scented item remains in the room during any given clean
- Preserve the cat's scratching surface, favourite hide, or an unwashed blanket during a change-of-occupancy-style clean for the same cat
- Give the cat a quiet space to retreat to while cleaning takes place
- Replace familiar items immediately after cleaning surfaces are dry

---

## 4. Cleaning Frequency Requirements

### 4.1 Daily Cleaning

The following must be completed:

- Clean and disinfect litter trays
- Remove soiled bedding and replace as needed
- Wash food and water bowls
- Clean up any urine, faeces, or vomit immediately
- Wipe down frequently touched surfaces (e.g. feeding areas)

### 4.2 Weekly Cleaning

Thorough cleaning and disinfection of:

- Litter trays
- Feeding stations
- Floors and surfaces in the cat's area
- Wash all bedding, blankets, and soft furnishings

### 4.3 Between Foster Placements (Standard 18)

When one cat leaves and before the next cat arrives, the foster room must be **thoroughly cleaned, disinfected, and fully dried** at change of occupancy. Foster carers must:

- Perform a full deep clean of the foster area (all surfaces, floors, skirting, walls within reach, shelves, scratching posts, carrier, feeding stations, and litter trays)
- Disinfect all surfaces, equipment, and carriers with an approved disinfectant at the correct dilution and contact time
- Replace or thoroughly wash all bedding, blankets, and soft items
- **Allow all surfaces to fully dry** before the next cat is placed — damp surfaces can harbour pathogens
- Observe a **minimum 24-hour rest period** between the deep clean and the next placement wherever operationally possible

The Fostering Coordinator will not schedule a new placement into a foster home until the Coordinator has confirmation that the change-of-occupancy clean has been completed.

---

## 5. Approved Cleaning Products and Chemical Storage (Standards 20, 21)

Foster carers must:

- Use pet-safe disinfectants approved by Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue (Anigiene or equivalent)
- Follow manufacturer instructions for dilution and contact time
- **Never** use phenolic disinfectants (including some household cleaners) — they are toxic to cats (Standard 20)
- Never mix cleaning chemicals
- **Store all cleaning chemicals securely** — in a locked cupboard, high shelf, or sealed container out of reach of cats, resident pets, and children (Standard 21)
- Store chemicals in their original containers with the manufacturer's label intact, following manufacturer instructions for handling and disposal
- Keep a dedicated set of foster-room cleaning equipment that is not shared with household areas

---

## 6. Litter Tray Hygiene

- Scoop litter at least once daily (more frequently for multiple cats/kittens)
- Fully empty, clean, and disinfect trays at least weekly
- Use separate trays for different litters where possible
- Dispose of waste in sealed bags

---

## 7. Food Preparation Hygiene

- Use clean bowls for each feeding
- Wash bowls with hot water and detergent after each use
- Store food in sealed containers
- Prepare food in a clean area separate from litter trays
- Always ensure that feeding bowls are away from litter trays
- Always ensure water bowls are away from feeding bowls and litter trays

---

## 8. Laundry Procedures

- Wash bedding and soft items at high temperature (ideally 60°C or above)
- Use pet-safe detergents
- Dry items thoroughly before reuse
- Keep separate from human laundry where possible

---

## 9. Infection Control & Isolation

### 9.1 Isolation Requirements

Cats must be isolated when:

- Newly arrived — until assessed by a vet and given at least first vaccine
- Showing signs of illness (e.g. diarrhoea, sneezing, discharge)
- Directed by the rescue or veterinarian

### 9.2 Isolation Protocols

- Use a separate room if possible
- Use dedicated equipment (bowls, litter trays, bedding)
- Handle isolated cats last
- Wash hands and change clothing after contact
- Crate if necessary for medical treatment

---

## 10. Personal Protective Measures

Foster carers should:

- Wear gloves when handling waste or cleaning
- Use protective clothing where appropriate
- Wash hands thoroughly after cleaning tasks

---

## 11. Waste Disposal

- Dispose of litter and waste in sealed bags in household waste
- Follow local council regulations for waste disposal
- Clean and disinfect bins regularly

---

## 12. Resident Pets

- Keep foster cats separated from resident pets unless approved and the isolation period/illness has elapsed and vaccinations have been given
- Do not share bowls, litter trays, or bedding
- Maintain vaccination and parasite control for resident animals

---

## 13. Reporting Concerns

Foster carers must report immediately:

- Signs of illness (e.g. vomiting, diarrhoea, lethargy)
- Parasites (fleas, worms)
- Any suspected infectious disease
- Cleaning or hygiene issues that cannot be managed

---

## 14. Compliance

Failure to follow this policy may result in:

- Review of fostering suitability
- Additional training requirements
- Removal of foster animals if necessary
