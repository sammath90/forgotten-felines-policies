# Privacy Notice

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

## 1. Who We Are

Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue ("Forgotten Felines", "we", "us") is a registered charity in England and Wales (Charity No. 1181190).

We are the data controller for the personal data we process in connection with our rescue, fostering, adoption, volunteer, and fundraising activities.

---

## 2. Contact for Data Protection Queries

**Data Protection Lead:** Sam Mathias  
Contact route: via Forgotten Felines official trustee channels (email/website/Facebook page inbox)

If you have questions about this notice or how we use your data, contact us using our published channels and ask for the Data Protection Lead.

---

## 3. Personal Data We Collect

Depending on your relationship with us, we may collect:

- Name and contact details (email, phone, address)
- Adoption/fostering application details
- Home check and suitability information
- Volunteer/trustee onboarding details
- Communications and enquiry records
- Donation/fundraising-related records
- Incident or complaint information
- Limited special category/sensitive data where necessary (eg safeguarding or health-related welfare context)

We only collect data we need for specific, legitimate rescue and governance purposes.

---

## 4. How We Use Personal Data

We use personal data to:

- Assess and manage adoption and fostering arrangements
- Coordinate cat welfare, transport, and veterinary care
- Manage volunteers, trustees, and role responsibilities
- Communicate with adopters, fosterers, supporters, and service providers
- Manage incidents, complaints, and safeguarding concerns
- Meet legal, accounting, charity governance, and regulatory obligations

---

## 5. Lawful Bases We Rely On

Under UK GDPR, we process personal data using lawful bases including:

- **Legitimate interests** (eg operating rescue services safely and effectively)
- **Contract** (eg pre-adoption/fostering arrangements)
- **Legal obligation** (eg charity governance, reporting, accounting requirements)
- **Consent** where appropriate (eg certain communications or optional uses)
- **Vital interests** in urgent welfare/safeguarding contexts where applicable

For special category data, we apply an additional lawful condition as required by law.

---

## 6. Who We Share Data With

We may share personal data with:

- Veterinary practices and clinical providers
- Trustees, coordinators, and authorised volunteers on a need-to-know basis
- Regulators or statutory bodies where required (eg Charity Commission, ICO, police, social services)
- Service providers supporting charity operations (eg approved storage or admin tools)

We do not sell personal data.

---

## 7. Communications Channels and Risk

Some charity communications currently use consumer messaging platforms.  
Where sensitive matters arise (eg safeguarding, complaints, personal data breaches), we will move handling to more controlled channels as soon as practical.

We ask all trustees and volunteers to follow minimum-necessary sharing and confidentiality rules at all times.

---

## 8. How Long We Keep Data

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purpose collected, legal obligations, and operational needs.

Retention periods and deletion approach are set out in our Data Retention Policy.

---

## 9. Your Rights

You have rights under UK GDPR, including:

- Right to be informed
- Right of access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure (in certain circumstances)
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to object
- Right to data portability (where applicable)

To exercise your rights, contact us and ask for the Data Protection Lead.

---

## 10. Data Security

We use proportionate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data from unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or disclosure.

Access to personal data is limited to people who need it for legitimate charity purposes.

---

## 11. Personal Data Breaches

If a personal data breach occurs, we will investigate and act promptly.  
Where required, we will report reportable breaches to the ICO within legal timeframes and notify affected individuals where legally required.

---

## 12. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we handle your personal data, please contact us first so we can try to resolve your concern.

You can also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/

---

## 13. Changes to This Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time.  
The latest version should be published in the charity's official document set and public channels as appropriate.
