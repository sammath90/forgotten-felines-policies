# Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

## Overview

This Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) documents all personal data processing activities carried out by Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue as required under **UK GDPR Article 30**.

As a data controller, Forgotten Felines must maintain and keep this record up to date. It must be made available to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) upon request.

**Data Controller:** Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue  
**Charity Registration:** 1181190 (England and Wales)  
**Data Protection Lead:** Sam Mathias  
**Contact:** Via official trustee channels (email / website / Facebook Page inbox)  
**Last Reviewed:** April 2026

## How to Read This Record

Each section covers one processing activity. For each activity, the record documents:

- **Purpose** — why the data is processed
- **Data subjects** — who the data belongs to
- **Categories of data** — what types of data are held
- **Lawful basis** — the UK GDPR legal ground relied upon
- **Recipients** — who data is shared with
- **Retention period** — how long data is kept (see also: Data Retention Policy)
- **Security measures** — how data is protected

## Processing Activity 1: Adoption Applications and Assessments

**Purpose:** Assess the suitability of prospective adopters, manage the adoption process, coordinate cat handovers, and support post-adoption welfare.

**Data subjects:** Prospective adopters and approved adopters.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Name, address, phone number, email address
- Household and lifestyle details provided in application forms
- Home check notes and suitability assessments
- Communications (messages, notes, decisions)
- Photos/videos provided by adopters (where submitted)
- Limited sensitive data where relevant to animal welfare (eg information about children or other pets)

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(b) — *Contract*: processing necessary for steps taken at the data subject's request prior to entering into an adoption arrangement.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: ensuring safe and responsible cat rehoming.

**Special category data (UK GDPR Article 9):** Where household information touches on health or family circumstance, Article 9(2)(g) — *substantial public interest* (animal welfare) — is applied proportionately.

**Recipients:**

- Trustees and coordinators involved in the assessment (need-to-know basis)
- Fosterer holding the cat (name and phone number only, once adopter approved)
- Veterinary providers where relevant to the placement

**Retention period:** 12 months from the date the adoption is completed, then securely deleted.

**Security measures:** Data held in official trustee channels and approved storage only. Access limited to involved trustees and coordinators. Fosterers must delete adopter contact details after handover unless welfare follow-up is required.

## Processing Activity 2: Fostering Arrangements

**Purpose:** Recruit, onboard, and manage fosterers; coordinate cat placements; support fosterer welfare and compliance with Forgotten Felines standards.

**Data subjects:** Current and former fosterers.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Name, address, phone number, email address
- Onboarding and agreement records
- Home and household details (capacity, experience, other animals)
- Communications and case notes related to cats in their care
- Vet visit and welfare records where fosterer is involved

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(b) — *Contract*: processing necessary for the volunteer fostering arrangement.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: managing the charity's rescue operations and cat welfare.

**Recipients:**

- Trustees and coordinators on a need-to-know basis
- Adopters (name and phone number only, for collection coordination)
- Veterinary providers where relevant

**Retention period:** Duration of the fostering relationship plus 12 months after involvement ends, then securely deleted.

**Security measures:** Data held in official channels only. Fosterers are required to handle adopter data in accordance with the GDPR Volunteer Policy and delete it once no longer needed.

## Processing Activity 3: Volunteer Management

**Purpose:** Recruit, manage, and coordinate volunteers (including home checkers, transport volunteers, and event helpers); maintain records for operational continuity.

**Data subjects:** Current and former volunteers.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Name, contact details
- Role and availability information
- Onboarding and agreement records
- Communications and activity records

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(b) — *Contract*: processing necessary for the volunteer arrangement.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: effective coordination of rescue operations.

**Recipients:**

- Trustees and coordinators on a need-to-know basis

**Retention period:** Duration of involvement plus 12 months after the volunteer leaves, then securely deleted.

**Security measures:** Held in official trustee systems. Access restricted to those managing volunteer coordination.

## Processing Activity 4: Trustee and Governance Records

**Purpose:** Manage trustee responsibilities, maintain Charity Commission compliance, record decisions, and fulfil legal governance obligations.

**Data subjects:** Trustees and committee members.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Name, contact details
- Role and responsibilities
- Meeting minutes and decisions (where personal data is referenced)
- Charity Commission registration and reporting records

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(c) — *Legal obligation*: Charity Commission registration and governance requirements.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: effective charity governance.

**Recipients:**

- Charity Commission (statutory reporting)
- ICO (where required)
- Trustees on a need-to-know basis

**Retention period:** Duration of trustee involvement plus 6 years (statutory governance records), then reviewed.

**Security measures:** Held in official trustee systems. Shared only with relevant parties as required by law or governance need.

## Processing Activity 5: Fundraising and Donation Records

**Purpose:** Process donations, manage Gift Aid claims, thank supporters, and meet statutory accounting and audit obligations.

**Data subjects:** Donors and fundraising supporters.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Name and contact details
- Donation amounts and dates
- Gift Aid declarations and eligibility
- Payment records (processed via third-party platforms; card/bank data not held directly)

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(c) — *Legal obligation*: HMRC Gift Aid and financial record-keeping requirements.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: managing charitable fundraising sustainably.
- Article 6(1)(a) — *Consent*: where donors opt in to future communications.

**Recipients:**

- HMRC (Gift Aid claims)
- Accountants or auditors where applicable
- Third-party fundraising platforms (JustGiving, Facebook Fundraisers — governed by their own privacy terms)

**Retention period:** 6 years from the end of the relevant financial year (HMRC requirement), then securely deleted.

**Security measures:** Donation records held securely. Third-party platforms used are subject to their own data processing terms. Gift Aid data handled in line with HMRC guidelines.

## Processing Activity 6: Cat Welfare and Veterinary Coordination

**Purpose:** Coordinate veterinary care, transport, and welfare management for cats in Forgotten Felines' care.

**Data subjects:** Volunteers, fosterers, and veterinary contacts involved in cat care.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Contact details of fosterers, transport volunteers, and vet staff
- Communications related to individual cat welfare cases
- Vet records and clinical correspondence (relating to cats, not people — but may contain incidental personal data of carers)

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: protecting animal welfare and coordinating rescue operations.

**Recipients:**

- Veterinary practices
- Fosterers and transport volunteers involved in the case
- Trustees coordinating care

**Retention period:** Until the cat's case is closed (adopted, transferred, or deceased), then deleted. Vet records may be transferred with the animal.

**Security measures:** Communications limited to official channels. Shared only with those directly involved in the cat's care.

## Processing Activity 7: Incident, Complaint, and Safeguarding Records

**Purpose:** Manage complaints, respond to welfare incidents, and handle any safeguarding concerns in accordance with legal and regulatory obligations.

**Data subjects:** Any individual involved in an incident or complaint (adopters, volunteers, members of the public, or children where relevant).

**Categories of personal data:**

- Names and contact details of those involved
- Written records of the incident or complaint
- Correspondence and outcome notes
- Safeguarding-relevant information (if applicable)

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(c) — *Legal obligation*: regulatory and safeguarding reporting requirements.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: protecting people and animals involved with the charity.

**Special category data (UK GDPR Article 9):** Where health or safeguarding data is processed, Article 9(2)(g) — *substantial public interest* — applies. Data is kept to the minimum necessary.

**Recipients:**

- Relevant trustees and the Data Protection Lead
- Statutory bodies where required (eg Charity Commission, ICO, police, social services)

**Retention period:** Until the matter is resolved, then reviewed. Special category data deleted promptly once the immediate need is met.

**Security measures:** Handled through controlled channels. Access strictly limited to those with a direct need. Sensitive matters escalated to more secure communication channels.

---

## Processing Activity 8: Public Communications and Social Media

**Purpose:** Promote the charity, share adoption updates, run fundraising campaigns, and communicate with supporters publicly.

**Data subjects:** Members of the public, adopters (with consent), and supporters who engage with public content.

**Categories of personal data:**

- Names and images where individuals appear in adoption posts or event content (with consent)
- Messages and comments received via public channels

**Lawful basis (UK GDPR Article 6):**

- Article 6(1)(a) — *Consent*: for identifiable adoption or event photos shared publicly.
- Article 6(1)(f) — *Legitimate interests*: charity communications and promotion.

**Recipients:**

- General public (via Facebook, Instagram, and other public channels)

**Retention period:** Permanent unless a removal request is received and granted.

**Security measures:** No personal details shared without consent. Photos containing identifiable people require explicit consent before publication. Happy adoption posts require adopter agreement.
