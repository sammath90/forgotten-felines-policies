# Trustee Code of Conduct & Governance Policy

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the standards, governance expectations, and accountability requirements for trustees of Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue.

It supports trustee duties under Charity Commission guidance and provides clear rules for attendance, conduct, decision-making, and role delivery.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all trustees, including officers and trustees who hold additional operational roles.

---

## 3. Trustee Duties and Core Standards

Trustees must:

- Act only in the charity's best interests
- Manage charity resources responsibly
- Act with reasonable care and skill
- Comply with the charity's governing document and relevant law
- Declare and manage conflicts of interest
- Respect confidentiality and data protection obligations
- Treat colleagues, volunteers, fosterers, adopters, and the public with respect

---

## 4. Role Ownership and Accountability

Each trustee will have named role responsibilities in the Roles & Responsibilities Register.

Trustees must:

- Understand and perform their assigned role(s)
- Complete role tasks within agreed timelines
- Escalate blockers early where they cannot deliver
- Support handover when stepping down from a role

### Mandatory Participation Rule

Being a trustee means you agree to:

- Turn up to trustee meetings, and
- Perform your assigned role.

If a trustee misses **3 trustee meetings**, their status as a trustee will be formally reviewed by the trustee group.

Possible outcomes include:

- Support plan and continuation
- Role reassignment
- Temporary suspension from trustee duties
- Recommendation to step down

---

## 5. Meetings and Decision-Making

- Trustee meetings will be held at an agreed regular cadence.
- Agenda and papers should be circulated in advance where practical.
- Decisions should be recorded in meeting minutes with clear actions and owners.
- Material decisions (financial commitments, policy approvals, safeguarding escalations) must be minuted.
- Trustee votes may be used where consensus cannot be reached.
- Chair holds a casting vote where required by governance practice.

---

## 6. Conflicts of Interest

Trustees must:

- Declare actual, potential, or perceived conflicts promptly
- Record conflicts in a conflicts register
- Withdraw from discussion/vote where appropriate
- Not use trustee position for personal gain

---

## 7. Behaviour and Conduct

Trustees must:

- Follow the Harassment Policy and Bullying Policy
- Communicate professionally in meetings, group chats, and public spaces
- Avoid defamatory, threatening, discriminatory, or abusive conduct
- Respect agreed communication channels for sensitive matters

Breaches may trigger governance review and formal action.

---

## 8. Safeguarding, Incident, and Regulatory Escalation

Trustees must:

- Escalate safeguarding concerns immediately via the Safeguarding Lead route
- Report serious incidents through the agreed Serious Incident process
- Escalate reportable data breaches to the Data Protection Lead without delay
- Support submissions to Charity Commission, ICO, or other regulators as required

---

## 9. Information Governance and Confidentiality

Trustees must:

- Use charity-approved channels for sensitive or personal data
- Follow GDPR Trustee Policy and Data Retention Policy
- Keep trustee documents secure and access-controlled
- Avoid sharing confidential information outside legitimate governance need

---

## 10. Breach Management

Possible responses to breach of this policy include:

- Informal warning and corrective action
- Formal warning recorded in trustee minutes
- Role restriction or removal
- Trustee status review
- Referral to regulators/statutory bodies where required

---

## 11. Induction and Annual Confirmation

- New trustees must receive this policy on appointment.
- Trustees confirm understanding and acceptance in writing.
- Trustees re-confirm annually as part of governance review.

---

This policy will be reviewed at least annually, or sooner after any major governance change or serious incident.
