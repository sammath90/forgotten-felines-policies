# Harassment Policy

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

## 1. Purpose

Forgotten Felines is committed to a safe, respectful, and inclusive environment for trustees, volunteers, fosterers, adopters, supporters, and members of the public.  
This policy defines harassment, sets expectations for behaviour, and explains how concerns will be reported, investigated, and resolved.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all conduct connected to Forgotten Felines, including:

- In-person meetings, events, home visits, and handovers
- Phone calls, text messages, email, and messaging apps
- Social media, public comments, and online groups
- Conduct between any trustee, volunteer, fosterer, adopter, or member of the public interacting with the charity

---

## 3. Definition of Harassment

Harassment is unwanted conduct related to a protected characteristic or any repeated/serious behaviour that violates a person's dignity or creates an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment.

Examples include:

- Threatening, abusive, insulting, or demeaning language
- Repeated hostile contact after being asked to stop
- Sexual comments, sexual advances, or inappropriate messages
- Bullying, intimidation, or deliberate exclusion
- Discriminatory remarks about protected characteristics
- Public or online targeting, shaming, or doxxing

One serious incident may be enough to constitute harassment.

---

## 4. Expected Standards

All people acting for or with Forgotten Felines must:

- Communicate respectfully and professionally
- Follow instructions from trustees and coordinators during visits/events
- Avoid personal attacks, threats, and discriminatory language
- Respect boundaries and privacy in foster homes and online spaces
- Raise concerns through formal channels rather than hostile confrontation

---

## 5. Reporting a Concern

Concerns should be reported as soon as possible to one of the following:

- Safeguarding Lead (for safeguarding-related incidents)
- Chair
- Incident Reporting & Complaints owner

Reports should include:

- Date, time, and location/platform
- People involved and any witnesses
- Description of what happened
- Screenshots/messages/photos where available

If there is immediate risk, contact emergency services first.

---

## 6. Initial Response

On receiving a report, the receiving trustee/coordinator will:

- Acknowledge receipt promptly
- Assess immediate safety risk
- Consider temporary protective actions (eg no-contact instruction, role suspension pending review)
- Log the incident in the incident record

---

## 7. Investigation Process

Investigations will be fair, proportionate, and confidential as far as possible.

Steps:

1. Assign a lead investigator (normally a trustee not directly involved)
2. Gather statements and evidence from all relevant parties
3. Review prior incidents or related complaints
4. Decide outcome based on balance of probabilities
5. Record outcome, actions, and rationale
6. Communicate outcome to relevant parties

Target timescale: conclude routine cases within 21 days where practical.

---

## 8. Outcomes and Actions

Depending on severity, outcomes may include:

- Informal resolution and behavioural expectations
- Formal written warning
- Restriction/removal from specific duties
- Removal from volunteer or fosterer role
- Escalation to trustees, regulators, or statutory agencies

Serious or criminal behaviour may be referred to police and/or the Charity Commission.

---

## 9. Malicious or Bad-Faith Reports

Reports made in good faith are supported, even if not upheld.  
Knowingly false or malicious allegations may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 10. Record-Keeping and Data Protection

- Records will be kept securely and only shared on a need-to-know basis
- Personal data will be processed under UK GDPR and the Data Retention Policy
- Incident records will be retained only for as long as necessary
