# Financial Policy

**Forgotten Felines Cat Rescue — Charity No. 1181190**

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out how Forgotten Felines manages income, expenditure, reserves, records, and financial controls to protect charity funds and meet governance obligations.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all trustees and volunteers handling charity money, payments, fundraising proceeds, reimbursement claims, and financial records.

---

## 3. Financial Roles

- **Treasurer / Accounts:** day-to-day bookkeeping, reconciliations, financial reporting.
- **Chair:** oversight and escalation for irregularities.
- **Trustee Group:** approval of budgets, major spend, reserves position, and annual accounts.
- **Fundraising Lead:** event income tracking and handover of records/funds.

Role ownership must match the Roles & Responsibilities Register.

---

## 4. Core Financial Principles

- Charity funds are used only for charitable purposes.
- Spending decisions must be transparent and documented.
- No one person should control authorisation, payment, and reconciliation end-to-end.
- Records must be complete, accurate, and auditable.
- Personal and charity funds/accounts must never be mixed.

---

## 5. Bank Accounts and Payment Controls

- Charity funds must be held in a charity-controlled account.
- Bank access must be limited to authorised trustees.
- At least two trustees must have account visibility.
- Payment evidence (invoice/receipt/approval) must be retained for each transaction.
- Trustee changes must trigger immediate access review and update.

---

## 6. Spending Authorisation Levels

Default limits (unless trustees approve exceptions):

- Up to £100: one authorised trustee approval
- £101 to £500: two trustee approvals
- Over £500: trustee group approval recorded in meeting minutes
- Any non-routine or sensitive spend: trustee group review regardless of value

Emergency welfare spend may proceed rapidly where cat welfare is at risk, but must be retrospectively documented and reviewed.

---

## 7. Expenses and Reimbursements

- Reasonable out-of-pocket expenses may be reimbursed where pre-approved or clearly within role duties.
- Claims must include original receipts/invoices and date/purpose.
- Claims should be submitted within 30 days where practical.
- A trustee cannot approve their own expense claim.
- Reimbursements must be recorded in the accounts ledger with evidence.

---

## 8. Income Handling (Donations, Fundraising, Adoption Donations)

- All income must be recorded with date, source, amount, and payment method.
- Cash income (if any) must be counted and recorded by two people where practical.
- Fundraising event totals must be documented and handed to Treasurer/Accounts promptly.
- Restricted funds must be recorded and used only for the intended purpose.

---

## 9. Lottery and Fundraising Compliance

- Lottery activity must follow licence conditions and legal requirements.
- Ticket sales, draw records, prizes, and proceeds allocation must be retained.
- The named Lottery Licence Holder must be current and aligned with licence documentation.

---

## 10. Budgeting and Financial Reporting

- Trustees should agree an annual budget (or annual forecast where proportionate).
- Treasurer provides regular financial updates to trustee meetings.
- Trustees review cash position, major commitments, and reserve sustainability.
- Material variances and risks are documented with actions.

---

## 11. Record Keeping and Retention

- Maintain ledger records for all transactions.
- Retain invoices, receipts, approvals, bank statements, and reconciliation evidence.
- Financial records must be securely stored in a charity-controlled location.
- Retention follows legal/accounting requirements and Data Retention Policy.

---

## 12. Fraud, Theft, and Financial Irregularity

Any suspected fraud, theft, or irregularity must be escalated immediately to the Chair and trustee group.

Immediate actions:

1. Secure evidence and preserve records
2. Restrict relevant account/payment access if needed
3. Record incident in governance log
4. Take legal/regulatory advice where required
5. Report serious incidents to Charity Commission where applicable

---

## 13. Annual Accounts and Reporting

- Prepare annual accounts in the appropriate format for charity size/type.
- Prepare Trustees' Annual Report and required annual return submissions.
- Ensure filings are approved by trustees and submitted on time.
- Keep copies of submitted returns and supporting records.
