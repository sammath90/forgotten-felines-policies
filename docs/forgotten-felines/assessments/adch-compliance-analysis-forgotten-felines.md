## 1. Overview

ADCH membership is explicitly open to foster-based rescues. The standards accommodate the model: Standards 43, 47, 48, 59, 60, 62 are written with foster homes in mind, and ADCH allows virtual home assessment under Standard 60. Forgotten Felines does not need physical premises to join. The foster room replaces the cattery unit for housing standards (Annex B). ADCH's approach is solutions-focused — they work with smaller rescues to meet standards rather than rejecting those with gaps.

This document is the **master compliance reference** for Forgotten Felines. It covers every standard in the ADCH Minimum Welfare Operational Standards (Standards 1–80, Annex A1–A17, Annex B1–B17, Annex C1–C9) and for each one records:

- Whether it applies to Forgotten Felines
- Current compliance status
- Which Forgotten Felines policy or register is the primary source of evidence
- Any outstanding action

Status legend:

- ✅ **Compliant** — policy language is in place and practice is aligned
- ⚠ **Partial** — policy covers the standard but action is still outstanding (e.g. register to be populated, sign-off to happen)
- ⛔ **Gap** — the standard applies and there is no adequate policy coverage yet
- ➖ **N/A** — the standard does not apply to Forgotten Felines (usually because it is dog-specific, premises-specific, or importation-specific)

## 2. Standards that do not apply to Forgotten Felines

Forgotten Felines is a cats-only, foster-based rescue with no physical premises and no importation activity. The following standards do not apply:

| Standard | Why not applicable |
|---|---|
| 8 | Communal feeding — not relevant in foster rooms where single-source groups share or are single-housed |
| 10 | On-premises food preparation facilities — no shared food preparation areas |
| 14 | First aid kit requirement for premises with more than 5 pens — no premises |
| 19 | Waste storage/treatment/disposal for premises — domestic waste streams used |
| 31–36 | Dog-specific environment, companionship, housing, and maternity standards |
| 53 | Transportation of dogs |

| 61 | Foster home overcrowding assessment for dogs |
| 63 | Dogs not to be fostered in homes where they become distressed alone |
| 64 | Dog exercise provision in foster homes |

| A1–A17 | Annex A dog housing conditions |
| C1–C9 | Annex C importation — no international imports |

## 6. Positive news for Forgotten Felines

1. **Foster-based is explicitly supported.** Standards 43, 47, 48, 59, 60, 62 are written with foster homes in mind. No premises are needed.

2. **Your policy suite already covers most of the written-procedure requirements in Standard 78.** The v2.0 animal-care policies map to Standard 78's eight areas almost perfectly; only the Emergency Plan is outstanding.

3. **Annex B is achievable in a normal home.** The space requirements (1.65 m² usable for one cat, raised surface, hiding place, washable surfaces) are met by a standard spare bedroom.

4. **The main remaining gap is *populating* the new systems, not designing them.** The schemas, templates, and programme structures now exist. The Phase 1 sprint is about filling them with real data: vet names, arrangement letters, training sign-offs, per-cat entries, home register rows.

5. **ADCH is solutions-focused.** Assessors will work with you to close remaining gaps rather than reject on a score.
