### fostering-policy.md

**Applicable ADCH standards:** 48 (fosterer training); 59–62 (foster home housing and assessment); 43 (pregnant/nursing queens); 44 (littermate housing); 65–66 (neutering); Annex B (housing conditions in foster rooms)

**Current coverage:** Cats kept separate from other household cats until fully vaccinated. Resident cats must be neutered and fully vaccinated before foster cat arrival. Foster cats remain indoors. **Gaps found:**

1. 🔴 **Standard 48 — Fosterer training programme not documented** (CRITICAL, major gap). ADCH Standard 48 requires written training programme covering 8 mandatory topics:
   - Animal welfare, including recognising poor welfare
   - Animal handling
   - Animal behaviour
   - Cleanliness and hygiene
   - Feeding and food preparation
   - Disease control
   - Recognition and first aid action for sick animals
   - New information on disease and inherited disease, legislation and behaviour
   
   Policy does NOT mention training at all. Gap: no reference to induction/training for new fosterers. This is a Critical gap — assessors will ask for training programme + individual records.

2. 🔴 **Standard 62 — Source separation ambiguous and potentially non-compliant** (CRITICAL, lines 19–22). ADCH Standard 62 states: "Cats from different sources shall not be mixed. Cats shall not be mixed with resident cats or other foster cats. There should be a room to which the cat is confined, which should be easily cleanable."
   
   Policy says: "A single foster room will only house: Maximum of 3 adult cats relinquished from the same household; A mother cat with her own kittens; A litter of kittens from the same household."
