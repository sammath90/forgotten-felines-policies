## Purpose

This register is the documented record of the veterinary practices nominated by Forgotten Felines under ADCH Minimum Welfare Operational Standards 13 and 51. Standard 51 requires every organisation to "nominate one or more veterinary practices or veterinary surgeons" to provide veterinary care. Standard 13 requires foster-based rescues without on-site isolation facilities to hold a "formal documented arrangement with a local provider such as a veterinary practice" for isolation of infectious cats.

The register is maintained by the Fostering Coordinator. Trustees review it at least annually. A signed arrangement letter is held on file for each nominated practice and referenced in the Services Confirmed column below.

---

## How the register is used

- Fosterers contact a nominated practice directly for routine care (health assessment, vaccination, neutering, treatment).
- The Fostering Coordinator confirms availability with the relevant practice before any non-urgent appointment.
- Invoices are addressed to Forgotten Felines and identify each cat by **First Name + Fosterer's Surname**.
- In an emergency where neither the Fostering Coordinator nor a Trustee can be reached, a fosterer has authority to contact a nominated practice directly under Section 10 of the Fostering Policy.
- If a cat requires isolation for an infectious disease, the Fostering Coordinator contacts the designated isolation practice (identified below) to arrange transfer under the Standard 13 arrangement.

## Review schedule

- The register is reviewed at least annually by the Fostering Coordinator with sign-off by a Trustee.
- A review is also triggered by:
  - A change of practice name, address, contact person, or opening hours
  - A change in services offered (for example, if a practice can no longer take isolation cases)
  - The end or renewal of a written arrangement letter
  - Any incident (clinical or operational) affecting the relationship with a practice

## Arrangement letter template

A signed arrangement letter should be obtained from each nominated practice. The letter should cover:

1. Confirmation of the practice–charity relationship and start date
2. Services the practice provides to Forgotten Felines (routine care, emergency care, isolation, etc.)
3. Primary contact person at the practice
4. Billing arrangement (invoice addressed to Forgotten Felines)
5. For the designated isolation provider: explicit confirmation of capacity to isolate an infectious Forgotten Felines cat, any conditions or capacity limits, and the notice/process required
6. Review / renewal arrangement (annual)
7. Signature of a veterinary surgeon or practice manager with authority to commit the practice, dated

The letter is stored with this register (electronic copy) and the original is held by the Fostering Coordinator.
