# Product Brief: Forgotten Felines Policy Suite

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Executive Summary

Forgotten Felines (Charity No. 1181190) is a foster-based cat rescue operating in South Wales, registered with the Charity Commission for England and Wales. The charity relies entirely on a network of volunteer fosterers who house cats in their private homes until adoption. As the charity has grown, the need to formalise, strengthen, and expand its policy framework has become clear.

This project aims to produce a complete, practical, plain-English policy suite covering operations, governance, data protection, safeguarding, social media, and fundraising compliance — fit for internal use by trustees and volunteers, and for sharing with adopters and the public. Critically, policies must be accompanied by acknowledgement mechanisms and role ownership to be effective.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Forgotten Felines operates without a physical premises — all cats are cared for in fosterers' private homes. This creates unique privacy and safeguarding risks that generic charity policies do not address. As the charity grows across multiple social media platforms (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, website) and operates a licensed online lottery, the policy framework must also cover digital conduct, fundraising compliance, and public-facing data obligations.

### Problem Impact

Without clear, comprehensive policies:
- Fosterers' privacy and safety are at risk
- The charity is exposed to GDPR breaches and ICO enforcement
- Volunteers and trustees lack consistent guidance for difficult situations
- Adopters and the public have no transparent view of how their data is used
- The charity risks non-compliance with Gambling Act obligations for its lottery
- The Charity Commission expects registered charities to have governance policies in place
- Without named role owners, escalation paths in existing policies are effectively dead ends
- Without signed acknowledgements, policies cannot be enforced against adopters or fosterers

### Why Existing Policies Fall Short

The current policies (Adopter Visit, Fosterer Visit, Data Retention, GDPR Trustee, GDPR Volunteer, Bullying) address specific scenarios but leave significant gaps:
- No incident reporting or complaints process
- No safeguarding policy (required by Charity Commission and relevant for Wales)
- No adoption policy covering the full process including home checks, refusals, and returns
- No post-adoption policy covering welfare checks and failed adoptions
- No social media policy beyond GDPR basics
- No lottery/fundraising compliance policy
- No public-facing Privacy Notice or Cookie Policy
- No trustee governance or code of conduct policy
- No Roles & Responsibilities Register — named roles referenced in existing policies but not assigned
- No signed acknowledgement forms — policies exist but adopters/fosterers don't formally agree to them
- Foster Coordinator role (held by trustees) is undefined in any policy document
- Existing policies do not distinguish between governance trustees and operational trustees

### Proposed Solution

Develop a complete policy suite for Forgotten Felines that:
- Strengthens and updates existing policies with clearer photo/privacy rules
- Fills all identified gaps with new, purpose-built policies
- Uses a two-tier structure: full governance policies + plain summaries and acknowledgement forms for end users
- Creates a Roles & Responsibilities Register as the connective spine of the whole suite
- Is written for action — every policy tells its reader what to do, not just what the rules are
- Is branded consistently with charity name and number (1181190)
- Includes a policy communication plan so trustees, fosterers and adopters are formally informed
- Is reviewable annually and version-controlled

### Key Differentiators

- **Foster-home specific**: Explicitly addresses the privacy risks unique to a home-based fostering model
- **Actionable clarity**: Every policy enables its reader to act confidently — fosterers to end visits, adopters to understand expectations, trustees to escalate correctly
- **Two-tier structure**: Full policies for governance, plain summaries and forms for people who need to sign and follow them
- **Role-anchored**: A Roles & Responsibilities Register names who owns what, so every escalation path leads somewhere real
- **Acknowledgement-backed**: Adopters and fosterers formally agree to relevant policies — creating an enforceable record
- **Multi-platform aware**: Covers TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and web specifically
- **Fundraising compliant**: Addresses Gambling Act obligations for the charity's licensed online lottery
- **Charity Commission aligned**: Meets governance expectations of a registered England and Wales charity

## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### 1. The Fosterer
**Profile:** Approximately 20 active fosterers at any time. A mix of experienced cat owners and families, varying in age and background. Some are also trustees; most are volunteers. They open their private homes to cats in the rescue's care and host adoption visits.

**Their experience of the problem:** They operate in their own home with limited formal guidance. They may feel unsure about how to handle difficult situations — an aggressive adopter, a child out of control, a request to photograph rooms. Without clear boundaries backed by policy, fosterers carry unnecessary personal risk.

**What they need:** Clear, confident guidance they can act on in the moment. Permission to say no and end a visit without second-guessing themselves. Assurance that the charity has their back.

**Success looks like:** A fosterer who feels protected, knows exactly what to do in any visit scenario, and has a signed acknowledgement from every adopter that rules were communicated in advance.

---

#### 2. The Adopter
**Profile:** A wide mix of the public — families with children, individuals, couples. Found primarily through Facebook, Instagram, and word of mouth. Motivated to give a cat a good home, but not always aware of the rescue's operating model or the privacy implications of visiting a private foster home.

**Their experience of the problem:** They may not understand why rules exist — why they can't photograph freely, why they must stay in one room, why a visit can be ended. Without clear upfront communication, rules can feel arbitrary or confrontational when enforced.

**What they need:** Clear expectations set *before* the visit, not during it. A simple, plain-English summary of what's expected of them. A formal acknowledgement they've agreed to the rules.

**Success looks like:** An adopter who arrives informed, respects the fosterer's home, and has a positive experience that reflects well on the rescue.

---

#### 3. The Trustee / Foster Coordinator
**Profile:** A small group of trustees who collectively govern the charity. Some also foster personally. Foster Coordinators are trustees with operational responsibility — matching cats to fosterers, coordinating visits, and acting as first point of contact when issues arise. Currently, responsibilities are held collectively rather than by named role owners.

**Their experience of the problem:** Without clear role definitions and documented escalation paths, incidents default to collective responsibility — meaning they can fall through the gaps. Trustees who also foster face dual obligations (governance policy AND fosterer policy) that aren't currently acknowledged or coordinated.

**What they need:** Named role ownership, clear escalation paths, and policies that distinguish between governance duties and operational duties. Confidence that if something goes wrong, there's a documented process to follow.

**Success looks like:** Every trustee knows their specific responsibilities. Every policy references a real named role. No ambiguity about who handles what.

---

### Secondary Users

#### 4. The General Public / Supporters
**Profile:** People who follow Forgotten Felines on Facebook, Instagram, or TikTok, or find them via word of mouth. May donate, share posts, enter the online lottery, or enquire about adoption. They interact with the charity's public-facing presence but are not part of operations.

**What they need:** Transparency about how their data is used (Privacy Notice), clear lottery terms, and confidence that the charity operates responsibly.

**Success looks like:** Public-facing policies that are easy to find and written in plain English — building trust in the charity's brand.

---

### User Journey

#### Fosterer Journey
1. **Onboarding** — Agrees to fostering terms; signs acknowledgement of relevant policies
2. **Day-to-day** — Cares for cats; receives guidance from Foster Coordinator
3. **Visit preparation** — Consults Fosterer Visit Policy; prepares designated room
4. **During visit** — Applies policy with confidence; escalates to coordinator if needed
5. **Post-adoption** — Deletes adopter contact details per data retention policy

#### Adopter Journey
1. **Discovery** — Finds Forgotten Felines via Facebook/Instagram/word of mouth
2. **Application** — Submits adoption application; data handled per GDPR policy
3. **Pre-visit** — Receives and signs Adopter Visit Policy summary
4. **Visit** — Meets cat in foster home; operates within agreed rules
5. **Adoption** — Takes cat home; welfare follow-up within agreed retention period

#### Trustee / Foster Coordinator Journey
1. **Role assignment** — Named in Roles & Responsibilities Register with defined scope
2. **Operational** — Coordinates fosterers, manages applications, handles incidents
3. **Escalation** — Follows documented escalation paths for complaints and breaches
4. **Annual review** — Reviews and re-approves policy suite each year
