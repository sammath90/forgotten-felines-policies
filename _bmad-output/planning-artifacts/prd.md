# Product Requirements Document - Forgotten Felines Policy Suite

**Author:** Sam
**Date:** 2026-03-21

## Executive Summary

Forgotten Felines (Charity No. 1181190) is a foster-based cat rescue operating in South Wales, registered with the Charity Commission for England and Wales. The charity operates without physical premises — all cats are cared for in volunteers' private homes. This creates privacy, safeguarding, and data protection risks not addressed by generic charity policy templates.

The charity holds an active lottery licence under the Gambling Act 2005 and operates across Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and a public website. Day-to-day operations — all internal communication between trustees and fosterers — take place on Facebook Messenger. This presents inherent GDPR compliance challenges that the policy suite must address honestly and practically.

This PRD defines the requirements for a complete policy suite: revising 6 existing policies and creating 15 new documents, including governance registers, acknowledgement forms, and public-facing notices. The suite serves trustees, fosterers (~20 active), adopters, and the general public — each with distinct needs, access levels, and obligations.

### What Makes This Special

Most charity policy suites assume a premises-based model and enterprise communication tools. This suite is built specifically for a foster-home model, where every visit takes place in a volunteer's private home and all trustee and fosterer communication happens on Facebook Messenger. It addresses the real compliance gap between GDPR ideals and the practical reality of volunteer-run charities operating entirely on consumer platforms — giving trustees and fosterers risk-aware, actionable guidance rather than unachievable standards.

Every document follows a two-tier structure: a full governance policy and a plain-English summary or acknowledgement form for the people who need to read, sign, and act on it. A Roles & Responsibilities Register anchors the suite — every escalation path leads to a named trustee.

## User Journeys

### Journey 1: Sarah the Fosterer — Her First Adoption Visit

Sarah has been fostering for Forgotten Felines for three months. She has a nervous tabby called Biscuit ready for adoption. A family has been approved and wants to visit on Saturday.

**Before the policy suite:** Sarah isn't sure what she's allowed to say about photography. She's uncomfortable when the family's child starts opening doors, but doesn't feel she has "permission" to say anything. She doesn't know who to call if things go wrong.

**With the policy suite:** Sarah received and read the Fosterer Visit Policy when she onboarded. She prepared the designated room, asked the family to sign the Adopter Visit Summary before they arrived, and knows she can end the visit at any time — and who to contact if she needs backup. When the child starts wandering, she calmly redirects them, backed by the policy she's already shared with the family.

**Climax:** The adopter's partner tries to take a photo that includes the room layout. Sarah says "I'm afraid photos are just of Biscuit — it's in the summary you signed." The adopter accepts it immediately.

**Resolution:** Biscuit goes to a great home. Sarah felt in control throughout.

**Capabilities revealed:** Fosterer Visit Policy, Adopter Visit Acknowledgement Form, fosterer onboarding, escalation contact clarity.

### Journey 2: James the Adopter — Applying and Visiting

James saw Forgotten Felines on Facebook and fell in love with a cat called Mittens. He applies through the rescue's process.

**Before the policy suite:** James receives no information about what to expect at the visit. He assumes he can take photos for his family who can't come. He feels the fosterer is being unfriendly when rules are explained on the doorstep.

**With the policy suite:** Before the visit is confirmed, James receives the Adopter Visit Summary — a plain one-page document explaining what to expect, what's not allowed, and why. He signs it digitally. He arrives understanding the rules and appreciating the professionalism of the rescue.

**Climax:** James asks if his daughter can hold Mittens. The fosterer says yes, and talks through how to do it safely — because the policy gives her confidence to engage positively rather than defensively.

**Resolution:** James adopts Mittens and posts a glowing review on Facebook.

**Capabilities revealed:** Adopter Visit Summary/Acknowledgement Form, pre-visit communication process, GDPR-compliant data handling of adoption application.

### Journey 3: Rachel the Foster Coordinator (Trustee) — Handling a Complaint

Rachel is a trustee and Foster Coordinator. An adopter contacts her on Facebook Messenger saying they felt the fosterer was rude and ended the visit unfairly.

**Before the policy suite:** Rachel isn't sure what the formal process is. She responds informally on Messenger, which creates a data trail outside official channels. There's no written record of what happened or what the resolution was.

**With the policy suite:** Rachel follows the Incident Reporting & Complaints Policy. She acknowledges the complaint, logs it using the standard form, moves communication to the official channel as quickly as possible, and reviews what the fosterer's signed policy says. She can demonstrate the adopter was informed of the visit rules before they arrived. The complaint is resolved and documented.

**Climax:** The adopter escalates. Rachel can show the signed Adopter Visit Summary and the logged complaint record. The matter is closed.

**Resolution:** The process protects both the fosterer and the charity. Rachel knows her role and what she's authorised to decide without consulting other trustees.

**Capabilities revealed:** Incident Reporting & Complaints Policy, Roles & Responsibilities Register, communication channel guidance (Facebook Messenger limitations), acknowledgement form as evidence.

## Domain-Specific Requirements

### Compliance & Regulatory

**UK GDPR (ICO):**
- Lawful basis for processing must be identified and documented for each data type
- Privacy Notice must be publicly accessible and meet ICO transparency requirements
- Data Retention Policy must specify retention periods per data category with documented rationale
- Breach reporting procedure required: ICO notification within 72 hours for reportable breaches
- Data subject rights must be addressed: right of access, erasure, rectification, portability
- No consent-based processing should rely on pre-ticked boxes or bundled consent
- Special category data (health, safeguarding) requires explicit consent or legal obligation basis

**Consumer Platform Compliance Gap:**
- All trustee and fosterer communication currently takes place on Facebook Messenger, which does not provide a Data Processing Agreement accessible to small charities
- Data shared on Facebook Messenger cannot be reliably deleted, audited, or exported — it sits within Meta's infrastructure outside the charity's control
- Policies must acknowledge this gap honestly: classify Facebook Messenger as a high-risk channel, define minimum-necessary sharing rules, and require migration to official channels for sensitive matters (complaints, personal data, safeguarding)
- Personal devices used for charity communication must follow the same retention and deletion principles as any organisational device
- WhatsApp and personal email may also be in use and carry the same risks — policies should cover all consumer platforms, not Facebook Messenger alone

**Charity Commission for England and Wales:**
- Trustees have legal duties: act in charity's best interest, manage resources responsibly, act with reasonable care and skill
- Trustee Code of Conduct must reflect these duties
- Serious incidents (safeguarding, data breaches, significant financial loss) may require reporting to the Commission
- Governance policies must be accessible and demonstrably followed

**Gambling Act 2005:**
- Lottery licence conditions must be met: prize limits, ticket pricing, proceeds to charitable purposes
- Records of lottery draws, ticket sales, prize distribution, and proceeds must be retained
- Participants must be 18+ — age verification or declaration required
- Terms and conditions must be published and accessible

**Safeguarding:**
- Charity Commission expects charities working with vulnerable people or children to have a safeguarding policy
- Named Safeguarding Lead required
- Policy must cover: recognising concerns, reporting procedures, escalation to statutory agencies (police, social services) where required
- Wales-specific: awareness of Welsh Government safeguarding guidance and Social Services and Well-being (Wales) Act 2014

### Strategic Option: Google Workspace for Nonprofits

Google offers Google Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Meet, Chat, Docs) free of charge to eligible UK non-profits via the Google for Nonprofits programme. Forgotten Felines may qualify as a registered charity (No. 1181190).

Adopting Google Workspace would significantly de-risk GDPR compliance by:
- Providing a Data Processing Agreement with Google (resolving the consumer platform gap)
- Giving all trustees official charity email addresses (@forgottenfelines domain)
- Moving communications off personal accounts and Facebook Messenger into auditable, controllable channels
- Enabling shared Drive storage for policy documents with access controls and version history
- Providing Google Chat as a compliant alternative to WhatsApp group chats

**This is acknowledged as a larger undertaking** — it requires domain registration, migration of contacts and communications, and trustee onboarding to new tools. It is not a prerequisite for the policy suite, but is noted as a strategic option that would make sustained compliance substantially simpler. The current policy suite should include guidance that makes the consumer platform situation manageable now, while noting Google Workspace as a recommended future step.

### Technical Constraints

- All policy documents must be accessible without specialist software (PDF or web-based)
- Acknowledgement forms must be signable digitally (e.g. typed name, email confirmation) or printable for wet signature
- Signed acknowledgement records must be retained per Data Retention Policy and stored securely — not in personal email or group chats
- Version numbering and review dates on all documents to support annual review process
- Documents must be readable by people with varying levels of digital literacy — plain English, no legal jargon

### Risk Register

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Fosterer data exposed via adopter photography | Enforced via Adopter Visit Policy + signed acknowledgement |
| GDPR breach via Facebook Messenger (primary channel) | Channel guidance policy; minimum-necessary sharing rules; migrate sensitive matters to official channels |
| Lottery non-compliance | Lottery & Fundraising Policy; named licence holder; draw records retained |
| Safeguarding concern not escalated | Named Safeguarding Lead; clear reporting procedure in policy |
| Trustee accountability gap | Roles & Responsibilities Register; named owners for all key functions |
| Policies exist but aren't enforced | Acknowledgement forms create signed record of agreement |
| Policy suite becomes outdated | Annual review process; review date on every document |
| Long-term platform compliance | Google Workspace for Nonprofits (strategic future option) |

## Documentation & Compliance Suite — Specific Requirements

### Project-Type Overview

This is a policy documentation project, not a software product. Requirements centre on document structure, accessibility, distribution, acknowledgement, version control, and storage — rather than technical architecture, APIs, or databases.

### Document Structure Requirements

Every policy document in the suite must include:
- **Document title** — clear, consistent naming convention (e.g. "Forgotten Felines — [Policy Name]")
- **Charity name and number** — Forgotten Felines, Charity No. 1181190
- **Version number** — e.g. v1.0, v1.1
- **Date of last review**
- **Next review date**
- **Named document owner / role responsible**
- **Scope** — who this policy applies to
- **Purpose** — why this policy exists (one paragraph)
- **Policy body** — numbered sections, plain English
- **Related documents** — cross-references to linked policies

### Two-Tier Document Architecture

Each policy that is end-user facing requires two versions:

| Tier | Audience | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full policy | Trustees, governance | Full document | Governance record, legal reference |
| Summary / form | Fosterers, adopters | 1-page plain English + signature field | To be read, agreed, and signed |

### Accessibility Requirements

- Plain English — no legal jargon; readable by someone with no policy background
- PDF and/or editable format (Word/Google Doc) for each document
- Printable for wet signature where digital signing not possible
- Consistent font, layout, and branding across all documents

### Version Control & Review Requirements

- All documents version-numbered from v1.0 at first publication
- Changes increment version number (minor edits: v1.1; significant rewrites: v2.0)
- Review date set at 12 months from publication on every document
- Annual review: trustees formally re-approve; version number updated even if no changes
- Change log maintained (date, version, summary of change)

### Distribution & Acknowledgement Requirements

- Fosterers: receive full policy pack on onboarding; sign Fosterer Acknowledgement Form
- Adopters: receive Adopter Visit Summary before first visit; sign before visit proceeds
- Trustees: receive and sign Trustee Code of Conduct on appointment
- Public-facing documents (Privacy Notice, Cookie Policy, Lottery Terms): published on website
- Acknowledgement records retained per Data Retention Policy

### Storage Requirements

- Single authoritative location for all documents (not scattered across personal drives, emails, or WhatsApp)
- Access-controlled: trustees can edit; fosterers can view relevant documents; public can access public-facing documents
- Recommended: Google Drive (especially if Google Workspace for Nonprofits is adopted)
- Backup: at least one additional copy maintained

### Implementation Considerations

- Documents should be produced in a consistent order: Roles & Responsibilities Register first (as it underpins all others)
- Each document should be reviewed by at least two trustees before publication
- A master index of all policies, versions, and review dates should be maintained
- Signed acknowledgement forms should be stored securely and separately from operational communications

## Functional Requirements

*The following requirements define what the policy suite must enable its users to do. Requirements are grouped by capability area.*

### Document Management

- FR1: Trustees can locate any policy document from a single authoritative index
- FR2: Trustees can identify the current version and review date of any document
- FR3: Trustees can access the change history for any document
- FR4: Fosterers can access the policies relevant to their role without requiring trustee assistance
- FR5: Public users can access public-facing documents (Privacy Notice, Cookie Policy, Lottery Terms) via the charity website

### Policy Content

- FR6: Each policy document states its purpose, scope, named owner, version, and review date
- FR7: Each policy document cross-references related policies within the suite
- FR8: The Fosterer Visit Policy gives fosterers explicit authority to end a visit unilaterally under defined circumstances
- FR9: The Adopter Visit Policy sets out rules for photography, conduct, room access, children, and data privacy
- FR10: The GDPR Trustee Policy identifies approved and high-risk communication channels and prescribes minimum-necessary data sharing rules
- FR11: The GDPR Volunteer Policy covers group chat conduct, photo sharing rules, and fosterer-specific data handling obligations
- FR12: The Social Media Policy covers conduct on Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok including content rules, crisis response, and personal vs. organisational posting
- FR13: The Safeguarding Policy identifies a named Safeguarding Lead and defines how to recognise, record, and escalate safeguarding concerns
- FR14: The Harassment Policy defines harassment, covers in-person and online scenarios, and sets out reporting and investigation procedures
- FR15: The Bullying Policy (revised) aligns with the Harassment Policy and suite standards
- FR16: The Incident Reporting & Complaints Policy defines the process for logging, investigating, and resolving complaints from adopters, fosterers, and the public
- FR17: The Lottery & Fundraising Compliance Policy documents the charity's licence obligations, age verification requirement, draw records, and prize/proceeds rules
- FR18: The Trustee Code of Conduct defines trustee legal duties, conflicts of interest, decision-making, expenses, and confidentiality obligations
- FR19: The Data Retention Policy specifies retention periods, deletion methods, and responsible roles for each data category
- FR20: The Privacy Notice sets out what data is collected, why, how long it is kept, and how data subjects can exercise their rights

### Roles & Accountability

- FR21: The Roles & Responsibilities Register names a specific individual against each key role
- FR22: The Roles & Responsibilities Register defines the scope of authority for each role — what each person can decide independently vs. what requires trustee group agreement
- FR23: The Roles & Responsibilities Register identifies which policies each role is responsible for maintaining
- FR24: Trustees who also foster can identify their dual obligations across both trustee and fosterer policies
- FR25: Every policy escalation path leads to a named role in the Roles & Responsibilities Register

### Acknowledgement & Consent

- FR26: Adopters can receive, read, and sign the Adopter Visit Summary before any visit takes place
- FR27: Fosterers can receive, read, and sign the Fosterer Policy Acknowledgement Form on onboarding
- FR28: Trustees can receive, read, and sign the Trustee Code of Conduct on appointment
- FR29: Signed acknowledgement records can be stored and retrieved as evidence of agreement
- FR30: Acknowledgement forms can be completed digitally or printed for wet signature

### Incident & Complaint Handling

- FR31: Trustees can log a complaint using a standard format capturing date, parties, nature of complaint, and actions taken
- FR32: Trustees can follow a documented escalation path for complaints that cannot be resolved at coordinator level
- FR33: Trustees can identify when an incident requires reporting to the Charity Commission, ICO, or statutory agencies
- FR34: The Safeguarding Lead can follow a documented procedure for escalating concerns to statutory agencies

### Data Protection & GDPR

- FR35: Trustees can identify the lawful basis for each category of personal data they process
- FR36: Volunteers and fosterers can understand what personal data they must not share in group chats or on personal devices
- FR37: Any person whose data is held by the charity can make a subject access, erasure, or rectification request and have it handled by the named Data Protection Lead
- FR38: The Data Protection Lead can follow a documented breach reporting procedure including ICO notification within 72 hours

### Governance & Review

- FR39: Trustees can conduct an annual review of the policy suite using a structured process
- FR40: Each policy can be updated independently without requiring the entire suite to be re-published
- FR41: Trustees can onboard a new trustee using the policy suite as the primary reference document
- FR42: The charity can demonstrate to the Charity Commission or ICO that governance policies exist, are current, and have been agreed to by relevant parties

## Non-Functional Requirements

*The following requirements define the quality standards the policy suite must meet — how documents should behave, not just what they contain.*

### Security & Data Protection

- NFR1: Signed acknowledgement records must be stored with access restricted to trustees only — not in shared group chats, personal email, or publicly accessible locations
- NFR2: Any personal data included in policy administration (e.g. complaint logs, acknowledgement records) must be handled in accordance with the Data Retention Policy
- NFR3: Documents stored digitally must be access-controlled: trustees (read/write), fosterers (read-only for relevant policies), public (read-only for public-facing documents)
- NFR4: The policy storage location must not be a personal account — it must be a charity-controlled location that survives individual trustee changes
- NFR5: Complaint and incident logs must be retained only for as long as operationally necessary and then securely deleted per Data Retention Policy

### Accessibility & Readability

- NFR6: All policy documents must be written at a reading age of approximately 14 years — no unexplained legal or technical jargon
- NFR7: All documents must be available in PDF format and, where possible, as editable documents (Word or Google Doc) for printing or adaptation
- NFR8: Font size, line spacing, and layout must be consistent and readable without specialist software or assistive technology requirements
- NFR9: Acknowledgement forms must be completable without requiring an email address or online account — a printed, signed version must always be an option

### Maintainability

- NFR10: Any single policy must be updatable without requiring changes to more than two other documents
- NFR11: The Roles & Responsibilities Register must be updatable (e.g. when a trustee changes) without requiring re-publication of any individual policy
- NFR12: Each document must include sufficient internal context that a new trustee with no prior knowledge can understand its purpose and apply it correctly
- NFR13: The policy suite must be fully reviewable by a small trustee team within a single meeting (approximately 2 hours) on an annual basis

### Legal & Compliance Quality

- NFR14: All GDPR-related documents must accurately reflect UK GDPR as it applies post-Brexit (not EU GDPR)
- NFR15: The Privacy Notice must meet ICO transparency requirements and be reviewed whenever data processing activities change
- NFR16: The Lottery & Fundraising Compliance Policy must accurately reflect the conditions of the charity's specific Gambling Act licence
- NFR17: The Safeguarding Policy must reference current Welsh Government safeguarding guidance and the Social Services and Well-being (Wales) Act 2014
