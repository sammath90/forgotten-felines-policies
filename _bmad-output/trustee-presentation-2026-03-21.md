# Forgotten Felines Policy Suite
## Trustee Briefing - March 2026

Charity No. 1181190

---

## Why We're Doing This

We've grown - and our current policies haven't kept up.

- Fosterers hosting visits have limited formal guidance on what they can and can't do
- Adopters aren't told the rules until they're standing on the doorstep
- We have no complaints or incident process
- We have no safeguarding policy - the Charity Commission expects one
- All our communication runs on Facebook Messenger, with no data protection agreement in place
- We hold a lottery licence with compliance obligations we haven't fully documented
- Policies exist but no one has formally agreed to them - meaning we can't enforce them

**The bottom line:** Without clear policies and signed acknowledgements, we can't protect our fosterers, the charity, or the people we work with.

---

## What We're Building

A complete, practical policy suite - written in plain English, built for how we actually operate.

**Two-tier structure:**
- Full governance policies for trustees
- Plain-English summaries and acknowledgement forms for fosterers and adopters to read, sign, and act on

**The backbone:** A Roles & Responsibilities Register - so every policy names a real person, not "the trustees"

**The goal:** Every fosterer signed up. Every adopter informed before they arrive. Every trustee knowing exactly what they're responsible for.

---

## Who It Protects

**Fosterers**
- Clear permission to end a visit without second-guessing themselves
- Know who to contact if something goes wrong
- Backed by policies the adopter has already read and signed

**Adopters**
- Know the rules before they arrive - not on the doorstep
- Sign a plain one-page summary in advance
- A better experience that reflects well on us

**Trustees**
- Named roles with defined authority - no more collective ambiguity
- Documented escalation paths for complaints and incidents
- Evidence for the Charity Commission that governance is in place

## The Facebook Messenger Problem

All our trustee and fosterer communication currently happens on Facebook Messenger.

**The gap:** Facebook Messenger has no data protection agreement for charities. Anything shared there sits in Meta's infrastructure - outside our control. We can't audit it, delete it reliably, or demonstrate GDPR compliance.

**Our approach:** We acknowledge this honestly. Policies will classify Messenger as a high-risk channel, set rules for what can and can't be shared there, and require sensitive matters - complaints, safeguarding concerns, personal data - to move to an official channel.

**The long-term fix:** Google Workspace for Nonprofits. Free for registered charities like us. Gives everyone a charity email address, shared document storage with access controls, and a compliant alternative to Messenger. Not required now, but strongly recommended as a next step once Phase 1 is complete.

**Order:** Roles & Responsibilities Register first - it underpins every other document

**Drafting:** AI-assisted drafts using constrained guard rails, trustees review, refine, and sign off

**Resource:** Trustee time only - no external costs

**Discipline:** Phase 1 complete before anything goes out. A half-finished suite is worse than none.
